Why it matters: With revenue of $69.6B and BMO raising its price target to $1,315, the earnings beat confirms Costco's membership model is resilient despite macro headwinds — and the company is suing the federal government to recover tariff duties already paid.

Amazon is a power player in the AI race with plans to spend $200 billion in total capital expenditures in 2026. Why it matters: $200B in capex positions AWS as the backbone of AI infrastructure — this is a long-term tailwind for AMZN, and at 29x earnings the stock is at a 10-year bargain valuation according to some analysts.

Apple Unveils iPhone 17e — $599 Starting Price

Apple unveiled the iPhone 17e with MagSafe charging, an A19 processor, and a 48MP Fusion camera, along with a refreshed iPad Air and a new MacBook Neo — all starting at $599. Why it matters: A new entry-level iPhone at $599 directly targets price-sensitive markets and could expand Apple's addressable base, particularly in India where premium models face stiff competition.

Alphabet's Cloud Backlog Hits $240 Billion

Alphabet's cloud backlog swelled by 55% quarter over quarter to $240 billion, with Google Cloud revenue surging 48% year-over-year to $17.7 billion in Q4 2025. Why it matters: A $240B backlog is a forward revenue visibility story — it means Google Cloud's hyper-growth has legs well into 2026 and 2027, making GOOG one of the clearest AI infrastructure plays in your portfolio.

Rocket Lab CEO & SVP Sell Combined $3M+ in Stock

Rocket Lab Corporation CEO Peter Beck sold 18,857 shares for $1,312,258 and SVP Arjun Kampani sold 23,711 shares for $1,706,006 — both in the same week. Why it matters: Two material insider sales totaling $3M+ from senior leadership following a strong earnings beat is worth monitoring. It doesn't necessarily signal a negative outlook, but the timing and combined scale is notable.

Berkshire Resumes Buybacks — What Abel's Era Looks Like

Berkshire Hathaway resumed stock buybacks on March 5, 2026, after a nearly two-year pause, and Greg Abel personally purchased $15 million worth of Class A shares. Why it matters: Buyback resumption + a $15M personal buy from the new CEO is one of the strongest capital allocation signals possible. Abel is putting his money where his mouth is.

AT&T Beats Estimates — So Why Is the Stock Down?

AT&T Inc. reported $0.52 earnings per share for the quarter, topping the consensus estimate of $0.46 by $0.06, with revenue of $33.47 billion also beating expectations. Why it matters: Beating EPS by 13% while the stock falls suggests results were already priced in — a classic "buy the rumor, sell the news" pattern.
